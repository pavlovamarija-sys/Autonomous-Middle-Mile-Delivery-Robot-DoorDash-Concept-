## Project Overview

Middle-mile delivery remains a significant cost driver and operational bottleneck for food platforms like DoorDash. This case study proposes a robotics-based solution to improve delivery efficiency, reduce variability, and scale operations sustainably.

The project examines:

* Market and competitive analysis
* User and stakeholder interviews
* Problem definition and opportunity sizing
* Rapid prototyping and design sprint process
* Engineering and operational considerations
* Product roadmap, KPIs, and success metrics
* Go-to-market and launch strategy

## Objectives

* Identify a high-impact opportunity in delivery logistics
* Validate user needs across merchants, dashers, and customers
* Design an end-to-end autonomous robot experience
* Develop a product strategy that balances feasibility, desirability, and viability
* Build a launch-ready proposal with KPIs, risks, and mitigation plans

---

## Research & Insights

* **Market analysis** revealed fast-growing demand for autonomous delivery, driven by rising delivery volumes and efficiency pressures.
* **User interviews** uncovered frustration with delivery delays, inconsistent timing, and operational inefficiencies in the middle-mile segment.
* **Competitive analysis** highlighted emerging robotics players but also gaps in mid-distance delivery automation.

Key Insight:

> Middle-mile automation offers significant potential for reliability improvements without disrupting the customer-facing delivery experience.
